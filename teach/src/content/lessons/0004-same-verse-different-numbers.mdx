## Bible idea: `book · chapter · verse` — then the twist

From Lesson 1 you already have the everyday address: **John 3:16** = book *John*, chapter *3*,
verse *16*. It feels like a universal coordinate. Mostly it is — but **not quite**, and the
exceptions matter.

Different textual traditions **number verses differently**. The same words can carry different
numbers depending on the tradition a translation follows:

- **Psalm titles.** A superscription like *"A psalm of David"* is counted as **verse 1** in the
  Hebrew numbering, but is **unnumbered** in most English Bibles — which shifts every later verse
  in that psalm by one.
- **Joel** and **Malachi** are divided into a different number of chapters/verses in the Hebrew
  vs. many English Bibles.
- **Revelation 12/13** and various **Hebrew/LXX** passages split verses at different points.

So a bare `chapter:verse` is **not a free universal key**. Two Bibles can both say "verse 2" and
mean different text.

## App idea: one Canonical Verse, reconciled by a Map

bible-codex resolves this with three pieces (all in `CONTEXT.md`):

- <span class="term">Canonical Verse</span> — a `(book, chapter, verse)` address in the **one
  chosen** canonical numbering (KJV-based, `av11n`). This is the single shared key everything
  anchors on.
- <span class="term">Translation Verse</span> — the *same passage* under a translation's own
  **native** numbering.
- <span class="term">Versification Map</span> — a per-translation table that **reconciles** that
  translation's Translation Verses to the Canonical Verse. It is the reason `chapter:verse` isn't
  a free key — and it stores a row **only where native and canonical differ**.

The crucial design move (and why this is safe): an **Anchor stores the *canonical* verse
directly**. So your marks key off the one shared numbering, and each translation's quirks are
absorbed by its Map — no mark ever depends on a translation's native count.

<div class="callout">
  <span class="tag">Teaching honesty — a known open risk</span>
  <p>The repo flags this plainly (<a href="https://github.com/riolly/bible-codex/blob/main/OVERVIEW.md">OVERVIEW §5</a>):
  the versification round-trip is <strong>UNVALIDATED</strong>. In the prototype, WEB &amp; KJV
  ≈ canonical, so the map had <strong>zero rows</strong> — the native↔canonical conversion it
  exists to perform was never exercised. It's safe to defer (anchors store canonical directly,
  and the map is rebuildable corpus data, not a locked seam), but it is honestly <em>not yet
  proven</em>. Validating it needs a genuinely divergent fixture — Psalm titles, Joel/Malachi,
  Rev 12/13.</p>
</div>
