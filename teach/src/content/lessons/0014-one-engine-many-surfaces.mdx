## Bible idea: depth, one step at a time

A reader wants to go **deeper** on a word — but the worst thing you can do to someone going deeper is
**drown them**. Open a typical study tool and you get a wall of tabs: lexicon, parsings, six
cross-references, three commentaries, all at once. The app's stance is the opposite — **revelation is
earned and incremental**. A translation appears **on hover**; a study note surfaces **one step at a
time**; the deeper lexicon waits until you ask. You grow into the depth instead of falling into it.

And notice what *drives* the pacing across every feature so far: the glow that knows you've read both
passages, the scrolls that open as you finish a leg, the themes drawn from your notes. They all lean
on one quiet thing — **what you've read, and how familiar you are with it.**

## App idea: one engine (journey state) + research mode over the lexicon

That "what you've read and how familiar" is the **journey state** — and it's the engine underneath
([WISHLIST](https://github.com/riolly/bible-codex/blob/main/WISHLIST.md), "the engine underneath"):

- **Journey progression** reads it to decide which scrolls open next.
- **Portal / clue pacing** reads it to decide when a word starts to glow.
- **Tag suggestion** reads it (and your notes) to propose themes.

> *One model, many surfaces. Worth building once, later.*

**Research mode** is the one surface that draws on a *different* already-built store: the **Phase-3
lexicon**. Translation-on-hover, incremental resource suggestions, deeper Strong's / morphology — every
bit of it is a **paced read** over the Original Word hub and the lexicon tiers you built in Module 3.
Nothing new underneath; just the anti-"too much information" pacing laid on top.
