## Bible idea: a library of scrolls, opened gradually

Most people feel one thing about the Bible: **overwhelmed**. It arrives as a single thick volume and
asks you to start at page one. But it didn't *begin* that way — it was a **library of scrolls**. The
**Journey** leans into that: you don't get the whole library at once. A **few scrolls open to start**,
each book keeps **its own bookmark** (your last place in it), only **so many scrolls are open at
once**, and **new books unlock as you finish** your current leg. The Bible becomes a path you walk,
not a wall you face.

Alongside that, a reader wants to mark passages by **how they relate** — *forgiveness*, *anger*, *the
Spirit of God*. These are **themes**, and tagging with them is a habit the app teaches early.

## App idea 1: Journey = reading progress + the earned-unlock gate

Look closely and the Journey introduces **nothing new** ([WISHLIST](https://github.com/riolly/bible-codex/blob/main/WISHLIST.md)):

- **One bookmark per book** is just **reading progress** — a single position stored per book.
- **Books unlock as you finish a leg** is the **same earned-unlock gate** the Portal used — a read of
  what you've finished, deciding what opens next. The **open-scroll limit** simply grows with it.

## App idea 2: themes & tags = the Anchor column-group, reused

A **tag** ("forgiveness" on this story) sounds like it needs its own machinery. It doesn't. A `tag`
row reuses the **same Anchor column-group** — `book · chapter · verse · word-index` — that a
**Mark** and a **Note** use ([ADR-0001](https://github.com/riolly/bible-codex/blob/main/docs/adr/0001-three-layer-anchor-model.md);
`tag` in [schema.dbml](https://github.com/riolly/bible-codex/blob/main/schema.dbml)). It anchors to
scripture exactly like every other user mark — so it ports across translations for free, the same way
your highlights do.

And **tag *suggestion*** — the app proposing themes for a passage — is a **read over your own notes**,
not a new database. It's the engine surfacing what you've already written; we'll meet that engine head-on
next lesson.
