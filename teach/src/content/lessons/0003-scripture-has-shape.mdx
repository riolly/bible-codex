## Bible idea: scripture has a literary *shape*

Open a Bible and the writing is not all one kind of thing. Scholars group it into a few broad
**literary forms** (genres), and they *look* different on the page:

- **Narrative / prose** — story and discourse. Flows as ordinary paragraphs.
- **Poetry** — Psalms, Proverbs, much of the Prophets. Built on **parallelism**: short paired
  lines (*cola*) that echo each other, traditionally set as **indented lines**, not paragraphs.
- **Headings &amp; titles** — section headings and, especially, **Psalm superscriptions**
  ("A psalm of David"). They label a passage but are not part of its running text.

The *same words* read differently depending on form. A psalm crushed into a justified paragraph
loses the parallel structure that *is* the poem. bible-codex's core literary bet is to **typeset
each genre to its own form** — so the reader sees poetry as poetry. (Beginner-friendly tour of
the genres: BibleProject's *How to Read the Bible*, in Resources.)

## App idea: two units — the Token and the Block

To typeset by genre, the corpus is stored as two things:

- A <span class="term">Token</span> is **one word- or punctuation-occurrence** in a single
  translation — the unit of storage, rendering, and hit-testing. Crucially, **whitespace is not
  a Token**: spacing is a presentation concern, never corpus data. (A KJV Token and a BSB Token
  are different objects — recall *spokes*.)
- A <span class="term">Block</span> is a **contiguous range of Tokens forming one literary
  unit** — a prose paragraph, a poetry line, or a heading — carrying a *genre* and an *indent*.
  Blocks are derived from **USFM** markers (`\p` prose paragraph, `\q1`/`\q2` poetry lines, `\d`
  Psalm title, `\s` section heading). **Blocks partition the stream**: every Token belongs to
  exactly one Block.

Headings are special: a heading Block's Tokens carry **no verse** (`verse = NULL`, never 0) and
sit *outside* the verse sequence — so in v1 they are **not anchorable**.

## The key insight: why a Token stream, not a list of verses

Here is the payoff, and it traces straight back to the Anchor decision (ADR-0001).

A single **verse often crosses Block boundaries** — one verse can be two poetry lines, or can
start halfway through a paragraph. So if you stored scripture *as verses* (a row per verse), the
verse units would be welded to the storage and you could never set the poetry as poetry.

The fix: store **one Token stream**, then lay **two independent overlays** over it —
**Blocks** (for genre-aware beauty) and **verses** (for addressing). Neither owns the text; both
just group the same stream.
