## Bible idea: your marks compose

On paper, marks don't just sit there — they **relate**. You box a phrase, write a note beside it,
then draw an **arrow from the note to a verse two pages over**. The arrow's whole meaning is "this
connects to that." A reader's marked-up Bible is a little graph of pointers, not a flat list.

The app keeps that. But it has to survive something paper never faces: the page underneath **moves**.
Change the font and lines reflow; switch translation and the words are *replaced*. On paper an arrow
is drawn at fixed spots; in the app, "fixed spots" is exactly the thing that would break.

## App idea: a scene graph anchored to scripture

Markup is a small **scene graph** of three element kinds ([ADR-0003](https://github.com/riolly/bible-codex/blob/main/docs/adr/0003-annotations-are-a-scripture-anchored-scene-graph.md)):

- <span class="term">Mark</span> — a decoration *on words* (underline, highlight, box, circle,
  strike). It has no location of its own; its position is **derived from the words it targets**. A
  Mark uses a **range** Anchor, so it can span a phrase — even *across a verse boundary*.
- <span class="term">Note</span> — your own free text, **pinned** to a scripture point plus an
  **offset** into the margin. It has its own identity, so it can itself be pointed at.
- <span class="term">Connector</span> — an arrow or line joining two <span class="term">Endpoints</span>.
  When it links two passages, it's a user-authored cross-reference.

The wiring trick: an Endpoint (or a Note's pin) binds to its target through a separate
<span class="term">Binding</span> record. Because the binding is its own record, **any element can
be a target** — including an arrow *from a Token to a Note*. This is the `boundElements`
back-reference idea borrowed straight from Excalidraw / tldraw.

## The inversion (and the Overview callback)

Here's what's borrowed from a whiteboard, and what's **inverted**. A whiteboard like Excalidraw
stores every shape at absolute **canvas coordinates** — safe, because the canvas never moves. Our
substrate *does* move: it reflows on a font change, re-paginates on a typography-preset change, and is
**replaced** on a translation change. So we keep the bindings and back-references, but **invert the
source of truth**: no element stores x/y — every position resolves from a scripture
<span class="term">Anchor</span> (plus an offset for a free-placed Note), computed at render.

  pixels. Same refusal, three times — and it's <strong>why your marks live through reflow,
  typography changes, and translation swaps with no migration</strong>.</p>
