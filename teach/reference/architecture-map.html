How bible-codex is shaped

Four loosely-coupled data worlds that touch only through two bridges, and one coordinate — the Anchor — that everything user-made hangs on.

The thesis in one breath

The Bible text is read-only and shipped with the app. Your marks are separate, yours, and synced. They never touch directly — they meet only at scripture coordinates. That separation is what lets text be re-imported and marks still survive.

Translations are spokes, never wired together

The three seams that are locked forever

The repo's discipline: only three things are "migration-fatal." Lock these, and don't over-design anything else.

  1. Anchors key off coordinates (verse + word-index + optional Original-Word id), never internal DB ids — so the corpus stays freely re-importable.
  2. Corpus = a token-stream + verse/block overlays, ingested from USFM (which carries the literary structure that makes genre-aware beauty possible).
  3. Annotations are a scripture-anchored scene graph, never canvas coordinates.

Everything else — sync backend, fonts, Markup styles, eraser rules, DB engine, exact translation set — is reversible. Don't bikeshed it. (OVERVIEW §4)
