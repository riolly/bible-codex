# bible-codex — Vision

A beautiful, local-first Bible that turns reading into **discovery**. Not a reference tool that dumps information on you — a companion that draws you deeper, one earned step at a time.

## Principles

**Beauty first.** Scripture is typeset to its literary form — narrative as prose, poetry as poetry — and can be read as a horizontal scroll (echoing the original) or vertically. Reading should feel like handling a fine physical book.

**A collection of scrolls, not a bound book.** The Bible arrives not as one overwhelming volume but as what it originally was — a library of scrolls. You open a book and scroll it to your place; each keeps its own. Which scrolls are open to you grows with your journey, so you are never handed the whole library at once.

**Challenge, not ease.** We don't make everything frictionless. Where other tools hand over a search box and a wall of data, we ask the reader to engage, learn, and remember — assisting *and* challenging, pushing one step past the comfortable.

**Revelation is earned and incremental.** Insight arrives a tap at a time — a gloss, then the Greek, then the meaning — never an overwhelming dump. The reader grows into the depth instead of drowning in it.

**Reading as adventure.** The reader is looking *for something*. Connections across scripture are treasures to be discovered, not footnotes to be skimmed. The app cultivates the feeling of a quest — of meaning hunted and found.

**Navigation by discovery, not search.** You move through scripture by following earned, meaningful connections — not by jumping via a lookup box. Getting there is part of the reward.

**Yours to mark.** Like a physical Bible, you draw, underline, note, and connect — and your marks live on the text itself, surviving every change of font, layout, and translation.

**Local-first, multi-device.** Everything works offline on your device; your reading and marks follow you between tablet and desktop. (No phone — this is for focused reading.)

## The thread we're pulling

Discovery becomes tangible through **Portals** (see [WISHLIST.md](WISHLIST.md)): connections that *light up only once you've earned them*. Read Genesis 1, reach John 1, and the word *"beginning"* begins to glow — ready to carry you back to where the story started, now read with new eyes. This is the adventure made mechanical: scripture as a world you explore, where understanding one passage unlocks another.
